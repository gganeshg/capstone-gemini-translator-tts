The Old Key
A long flight of weathered steps led to a hollow wooden door with rusty numbers. Inside, the room was simple: a lumpy bed and faded wood paneling. But from the window, the seashore beckoned, and we wandered down to feel the sand between our toes. We returned to that orange-shingled eyesore repeatedly until the burgeoning resort finally tore it down.
Forty years later, my husband still periodically sends me short emails that simply declare the time: 11:08.
"I love you, too," I write back, a quiet smile on my face, remembering that simple room and the countless memories it held.
